Teriyaki Chicken Thighs

Serves 2 for dinner + preps 3 bowl lunches35 min + marinating
Week 2 — Sunday (Prep Day)

Ingredients

  • Bone-in skin-on chicken thighs — 4–6 (double batch)
  • — Teriyaki marinade —
  • Soy sauce — ¼ cup
  • Honey — 2 tbsp
  • Garlic — 3 cloves, minced
  • Fresh ginger — 1 tsp, grated
  • Rice vinegar — 1 tbsp
  • — Dinner side —
  • Bok choy — 2–3 heads, halved
  • Olive oil, salt, pepper
  • — Lunch bowls —
  • Persian cucumber — sliced
  • Scallions — sliced

Steps

  1. Mix soy sauce, honey, garlic, ginger, and rice vinegar. Coat thighs and marinate in the fridge (Sunday morning works great — 8–10 hrs is ideal).
  2. Air fry at 400°F for 22–25 min, skin-side up, until caramelized and internal temp hits 175°F. Honey burns fast — drop to 375°F in the last few minutes if the skin looks very dark.
  3. Toss broccolini halves in olive oil, salt, pepper. Roast cut-side up at 425°F for 10–12 min until edges char.
  4. Serve 1–2 thighs with the broccolini for dinner.
  5. Slice remaining thighs and refrigerate for lunch bowls.
Don't use leftover marinade as a sauce — it had raw chicken in it. If you want extra sauce for the bowls, set a small amount aside before adding the chicken.
