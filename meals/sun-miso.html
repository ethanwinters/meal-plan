Miso Chicken Breast + Roasted Brussels Sprouts

Serves 2 for dinner + batch-cooks thighs for 3 bowl lunches40 min total
Week 4 — Sunday (Prep Day)

Ingredients

  • Chicken breasts, boneless skinless — 2 (dinner)
  • Bone-in skin-on chicken thighs — 4 (batch-cooked for lunch bowls)
  • White miso paste — 2 tbsp
  • Soy sauce — 1 tbsp
  • Rice vinegar — 1 tbsp
  • Fresh ginger — 1 tsp, grated
  • Garlic — 2 cloves, minced
  • Brussels sprouts — 1½ lbs, halved
  • Olive oil, salt, pepper

Steps

  1. Whisk together miso, soy, rice vinegar, ginger, and garlic. Coat the breasts and the thighs. Marinate 30+ min.
  2. Air fry the breasts at 375°F for 16–20 min, flipping once, to 160–165°F. Miso browns fast — drop to 350°F in the last few minutes if it darkens. Rest 5 min.
  3. Air fry the thighs at 400°F for 22–25 min, skin-side up, to 175°F — these are for the lunch bowls.
  4. Toss Brussels sprouts (cut-side down) in olive oil, salt, pepper. Roast at 425°F for 22–25 min until cut sides are deeply caramelized.
  5. Dinner: a breast each with half the Brussels sprouts.
  6. Slice the thighs and refrigerate the remaining sprouts for the Asian bowls.
Miso burns faster than a plain marinade — watch the breasts and drop the temp if they darken. Cut-side down is non-negotiable for the sprouts — that's where the caramelization happens. Breast pulls at 160°F; thigh is fine to 175°F.
